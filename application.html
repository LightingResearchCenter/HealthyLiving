CS Chart

The CS chart is given as a reference to understand circadian design, and includes a time schedule of lighting exposure durations unique for varying user groups. The time range associates with a target CS level. The EV values (vertical illuminance) correspond with how many lux at the eye are needed to reach the target CS for that time of day. EH values (horizontal illuminance) are calculated based on the vertical to horizontal illuminance ratio of the fixture(s) used in the design. EV and EH values will vary depending on which CCT is chosen. For example, a 3000 K has less of an impact on the circadian system therefore requiring more light at the eye to reach a target CS of 0.3 when compared to 6500 K. Output percentage of the system is based on a linear dimming relationship of the reducing EV values, assuming the highest target for the day is at 100% output. Initial lumens and wattage per fixture are included and calculated based on how much is needed to reach the highest light levels in the design. Generally, cooler white light sources (6500 K and 5000 K) will require less initial lumen output and wattage since it requires less light getting to the eye than a warmer light source (2700 K and 3000 K). LPD (Lighting Power Density) is given for reference to energy consumption of the design. LPD is calculated based on the highest lumen output for the design, amount of fixtures, CCT multiplier, room area. This chart is meant to be used as a tool when implementing a circadian design. The time of day and associated CS targets are a guideline, and the associated values in the chart give an idea of how certain fixture types perform. All of the components will change due to all lighting fixtures having various characteristics. Horizontal illuminance measurements were taken on work planes, 2’ 6” AFF, and vertical illuminance measurements were taken at seated eye height, 4’ AFF.

Calculations are based on typical room reflectances of ceiling, walls, and floor being 0.8, 0.5, and 0.2 respectively.
