Room Description

A common area in a senior care facility is where residents can spend most of their day. This 1550 sf common area is divided into two areas for dining and activities. The dining area has round tables with chairs and the activity area has sofas, side tables, and rectangular tables with chairs for recreational use. From eating meals in the dining area, relaxing on the couch, or sitting at the activity tables, designs should aim to reach desired CS levels in these locations.

The lighting design consists of twenty-four 2x4 “center basket” troffers that are spaced 8’x8’ on center within the space.

Assumptions
Room reflectances:

A room’s finishing material and color can change perception of space as well as reflect or absorb light to affect how much gets to the eye. Reflectances are based on a percentage of how much light is reflected off a surface.

Ceiling: 80% (0.8)
Walls: 50% (0.5)
Floor: 20% (0.2)

Height of illuminance calculation points:

When determining how light performs in a space, it is important to know the height at which people’s eyes will receive light (EV) and the height at which tasks are being done (EH).

Horizontal illuminance (EH): 2’-6” AFF
Vertical illuminance (EV): 4’-0” AFF

Dimming system:

Knowing how your dimming system operates is important to determine what percent output your lights should be as brightness levels vary throughout the day.

Linear dimming system

Light Loss Factors:

When determining how light fixtures perform over time, it is important to know the depreciation over time. With light loss factors accounted for, light received from a fixture will decrease over time.

No light loss factors accounted for

Relationship between lumen output and wattage:

Changing the lumen output of a fixture will change how much light is in a space. Lumen output from a fixture is important to know achieve light levels to determine energy usage, wattage must also be known.

Linear relationship between fixture lumen output and wattage
